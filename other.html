  • How Fertile Am I?

    • How fertile am I?
    • Once the couple decides to start trying for the baby, the most obvious question is: How quickly is this possible? Certain things need to be kept before you start trying for baby, as longer unsuccessful trials brings frustration:
      • Having sex in the most fertile part of female partner's menstrual period (during ovulation) is most essential
      • Ovulation usually occurs mid cycle. More precisely, ovulation usually occurs 14 days before the onset of bleeding
      • Ovulation can be upset and delayed by many factors, e.g., sickness, alcohol, travel, stress
      • According to Human Fertilisation & Embryology and Authority (HEFA) the statistics show following:
        • 30% of couples conceive within a month of starting to try for a baby
        • 75 % conceive within six months
        • 90 % conceive within a year
        • 95% conceive within two years
    • However if no pregnancy occurs after two years of regular, unprotected sex then a medical opinion is suggested:
      • At this stage the fertility of both partners is checked
      • Tests can be done to find the problem and help the couple
      • The initial assessment is done by the doctor by asking questions regarding lifestyle, general health and medical history
      • This assessment is followed by simple tests like
      • Ovulation (egg release) test for women
      • Blood tests to check for hormonal problems such as PCOS and premature menopause for women
      • Screening for rubella (German measles) for women
      • Sperm count test for men
      • Chlamydia test
      • If the tests do not reveal anything negative and the female partner is young (less than 30) then the doctor will suggest to keep trying to conceive (6 months or more)
      • If the tests reveal something negative, detailed analysis is done to find the exact problem and doctors suggest the therapies or alternative ways by which couple can attain parenthood

  • Semen Analysis

    • Semen analysis comprises a set of descriptive measurements of spermatozoa and seminal fluid parameters that help to estimate semen quality
    • Conventional semen analysis includes measurement of particular aspects of spermatozoa such as concentration, motility and morphology and of seminal plasma. Quantification and identification of non-spermatozoidal cells and detection of antisperm antibodies are also a part of basic semen analysis
    • Time of abstinence (no release of semen) before doing semen analysis is 3-5 days.
    • World Health Organization (WHO) has given the following parameters to be checked in semen analysis
      • Standard Tests Normal Values
      Volume 2.0 ml or more
      pH 7.2-7.8
      Sperm concentration 20x106 spermatozoa/ml or more
      Total sperm count 40x106 spermatozoa or more
      Motility 50% or more with forward progression or 25% or more with rapid progression within 60 min after collection
      Morphology 30% or more with normal morphology
      Vitality 75% or more live
      White blood cells Fewer than 1x106/ml
      IMMUNOLOGICAL TESTS
      Immunobead test Fewer than 20% spermatozoa with adherent particles
      MAR test Fewer than 10% spermatozoa with adherent particles

  • Endometrial Scan

    • Endometrial is the lining of the womb and needs to be 7mm or above for a pregnancy to be able to implant
    • This scan is important for the couple who are trying in-vitro fertilization (IVF). It is guidance for the timing of IVF
    • It helps to estimate time of ovulation and to track the changes in response to fertility treatment
    • This scan is normally performed at 1 - 2 day intervals from about day nine of female's menstrual cycle – day one is measured as first day of bleeding
    • A long narrow probe is inserted into the vagina so that the appearance and thickness of the womb's lining can be assessed

  • Follicle Tracking Scan

    • Measures the size of any active follicle and helps to predict when ovulation will occur
    • Egg grows within a follicle. Follicle matures and grows in size to 20 mm on 13 or 14th day of cycle and ruptures to release egg
    • This tracking is carried out to time the intercourse with the ovulation or to schedule artificial insemination processes along with the ovulation
    • A long narrow probe is inserted a small distance into the vagina and the ultrasound signal is focused on the ovaries
    • Any growing follicles are measured, as when the follicles reach a certain size estimate can be made regarding the ovulation time

  • Gynaecology Care

    • Gynecology is the medical practice dealing with health of female reproductive system
    • Gynecological examination is thorough and extensive, more so than a routine physical exam

    • The main conditions taken care by gynecologists are:

    • Cancer and pre-cancer of reproductive systems like ovaries, uterus, cervix, and vagina
    • Amenorrhoea: It is the absence of menstrual period in a woman of reproductive age. There can be many reasons for this including congenital absence of uterus, failure of ovary to receive or maintain eggs, genetic diseases, pregnancy, eating disorders, ovulation disorders or premature ovarian failure
    • Dysmenorrhea: It is the pain during menstruation which hampers the daily activities. It is synonymous with menstrual cramps and can precede menstrual cycle by many days or may accompany it. Disease or structural abnormality within or outside uterus may also cause dysmenorrheal
    • Menorrhagia: Menorrhagia is an abnormally heavy and prolonged menstrual period at regular intervals
      • Diagnosis can be done by Pelvic and rectal examination, Pap smear, Pelvic ultrasound scan for identifying structural abnormalities, endometrial biopsy to exclude endometrial cancer or atypical hyperplasia and hysteroscopy
      • Based on the diagnosis the treatment would be designed by the gynecologist
    • Infertility: Inability of the couple to conceive. Problem may be because of either partner or both. Tests can be done to find the cause and suggesting cure and alternatives
    • Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS): Common female endocrine disorder. Both genes and environment contribute to PCOS. The principal features are anovulation, resulting in irregular menstruation, amenorrhea, and ovulation-related infertility. Obesity, exacerbated by poor dietary choices and physical inactivity, worsens PCOS in susceptible individuals
      • The consultant will need to assess the reason by ultrasound and few hormone tests to establish a diagnosis. Treatment depends if fertility is desired or not and the success rate depends on response to treatment, generally speaking, response to treatment is good
    • Endometriosis: Cells from the lining of the womb (uterus) grow in other areas of the body. This can lead to pain, irregular bleeding, and infertility
      • Diagnosis: Pelvic scan, transvaginal ultrasound and pelvic laparoscopy.
      • Based on diagnosis the gynecologist will suggest treatment
    • Fibroids: Noncancerous (benign) tumors that develop in the womb (uterus), a female reproductive organ. As long as a woman with fibroids is menstruating, a fibroid will probably continue to grow, usually slowly
      • Diagnosis: Gynecologist performs pelvic exam to check if there is any change in the shape of uterus. Ultrasound done to confirm diagnosis of fibroids
      • Treatment: Depends on age, general health, signs and symptoms, types of fibroids
      • Birth control pills to help control heavy periods
      • Intra uterine devices to release hormone Progestin to control heavy bleeding
      • Iron supplements and pain killers

    • Early Pregnancy Scan
    • Ideally 3 scans should be performed during the course of pregnancy
    • The first scan is usually in early pregnancy, around 6 - 9 weeks. It confirms following:
      • Location of pregnancy
      • To find singleton or multiple pregnancy
      • To confirm presence of fetal heart activity
      • To confirm age of pregnancy to estimate the due date
    • Thin probe is introduced into the vagina to enable better visualization of deeper organs


    • Fetal Anomaly Scan (FAS)
    • It is the detailed ultrasound scan at about 20 weeks of pregnancy to see if the baby is developing properly
    • Majority of babies are normal, but there is rare chance (irrespective of age) to deliver a baby with abnormal physical or mental problem. Such abnormalities can be ruled out by FAS
    • Includes examination of the skull, face, palate, neck, chest, heart, stomach, diaphragm, bowel, kidney's, umbilicus, bladder, upper and lower limbs. In addition, the heartbeat, placental position and fetal growth are checked
    • If the scan suggests a problem, couple is informed about it so that they can discuss immediately with the consultant specialist in fetal medicine
    • Approximately 90% of abnormalities are detected in this scan

  • Reproductive Immunology

    • Introduction
    • It is the study of interaction between immune systems (of mother and fetus) and the components related to immune system
    • The mother's immune system does not show any negative immune response against the fetus and placenta normally
    • Sometimes immunological responses of mother against fetus or placenta could be cause of infertility
    • These immune responses could lead to miscarriages and failure of in-vitro fertilization procedures. Mother's antibodies treat fetus as foreign component (does not recognize) and lead to miscarriage
    • Failure to conceive is sometimes due to immunological problems that can lead to very early rejection of embryo

    • Immune System Components Important in Reproductive System
    • These antibodies and immune system components can be one of the causes of the miscarriage. But with advances in the knowledge of immune system and reproductive system physiology these can be these immune reactions can be prevented
    • Some of the treatments are simple and can be performed by taking simply a tablet or infusions, but some are little complex and require proper guidance and treatment from doctor
      • Antibody/Immune system component responsible for miscarriage Treatment
      Antiphospholipid antibodies Aspirin and Heparin (blood thinner)
      Antinuclear antibodies Prednisolone (corticosteroid)
      Natural killer cells Immunoglobulin G infusion
      Cytotoxic B-cells Immunoglobulin G infusion
      Blocking (protective) antibodies paternal white cell immunizations and IVIg
      Antithyroid antibodies Intravenous immune globulin (IVIg) before the IVF transfer
